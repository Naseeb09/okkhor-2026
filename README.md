**Okkhor** is an interactive engineering substrate designed to map the structural and historical evolution of the Bangla script. Moving beyond static documentation, the system utilizes motion physics and generative design to treat the Bengali alphabet as a dynamic, evolving architecture.

Designed with a deep emerald palette and high-end industrial minimalism.

## 🛠 Engineering Modules

* **Evolutionary Anatomy**: A discovery engine visualizing character transformation. Triggers real-time transitions from 3rd-century Brahmi ancestors through Proto-Bengali to modern Eastern Nagari.
* **The Heritage Timeline**: A reactive vertical sequence documenting script milestones from 1000 AD to the 1952 Language Movement.
* **Generative Calligraphy**: Procedural synthesis of Bangla strokes into digital art patterns.
* **Borno Stack (Physics Engine)**: A tactile experiment assigning mass, gravity, and collision boxes to glyphs, enabling physical interaction with "glass and stone" typography.

## 💻 Technical Stack

* **Framework**: Next.js (App Router)
* **Logic**: TypeScript

* **Styling**: Tailwind CSS (Custom Glassmorphism Filters)
* **Motion**: Framer Motion (State-aware morphing & gesture transitions)

### Infrastructure

* **Deployment**: Vercel
